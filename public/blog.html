Understanding EWC Codes: A Simple Guide for UK Businesses

Proper waste classification is essential for legal compliance and environmental safety. EWC codes categorize waste into specific types, helping businesses dispose of it correctly.

  • EWC codes are 6-digit numbers, e.g., 17 01 01 for concrete.
  • They help ensure your waste is handled by authorized facilities.
  • Misclassification can result in fines and environmental harm.

Tip: Always consult official WM3 guidance for accurate classification.

Top Tips for Finding the Right Waste Facility in the UK

Not all waste facilities are the same. This guide helps you locate a licensed, permitted site efficiently.

  • Use WasteLocate to search by EWC code and location.
  • Confirm permits via the Environment Agency public register.
  • Some waste types, especially hazardous waste, require specialist handling.
  • Keep records of all transfers for Duty of Care compliance.

Pro Tip: Schedule waste collections in advance to avoid delays.

Duty of Care in Waste Management: What UK Businesses Need to Know

UK businesses are legally responsible for the safe storage, transfer, and disposal of waste. Key responsibilities include:

  • Correctly describe and classify waste.
  • Transfer waste only to authorized persons.
  • Keep Waste Transfer Notes for at least 2 years.
  • Regularly verify that your chosen facilities hold valid permits.

Reminder: Failing Duty of Care can lead to fines, prosecution, and environmental damage.
